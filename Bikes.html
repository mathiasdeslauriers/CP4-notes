Bikes.. What can I say? they're way too expensive, they take a lot of maintenance. Then again, they're fun. You will most likely end up spending thousands of dollars on parts or a brand new spankin' bike. Have fun. This is the list that i think can make you make your decision making easier.

Enduro/All Mountain.

If you want a bike that can do everything, like climbs, descents on downhill tails, look no further. These bikes are more comfortable because they have a shock and they can take bigger impacts.

It has a 170 mm Yari RC fork. This is a good for, pleanty of support, but its nothing like the Lyric which is much better.
It has a rockshox monarch plus Very good with adjustable compression, air, and rebound. It is a piggy shock, meaning that there is an extra air canister.
For this price i am happy to see a capable dropper post.
It is an all rounder good bike.

Norco Sight A3 This bike has pretty good value for the spec especially if you live in a country where our money is worth less
It has a Rockshox Deluxe RT with debponair. It allows for 140 mm of travel
It has a Rockshox revelation RC with 150mm of travel. It isnt the best fork out there, but its not horrible.
Im happy enough with the Sram NX 1 x 11. It provides smooth shifting.
Again very happy to have a dropper post
3600$
Jeffsy 27 Al YT This bike has by far the best value for the money
Its the only bike with Fox suspension, which i like a lot.
This bike has very capable brakes with the Guide Brakes.
The Maxxis High rollers are very capable, and are great in wet or dry weather.
3200$ USD
Giant Reign 2 For me this is the bike with the less value for the spec in the list.
This bike has a rockshox shock like the others. But is doesn't have a debonair. Its less performant
The Shimano Deore 1x 10 has less range from the rest of the bikes. Its shifting isn't as smooth.
The Shimano deore hydrolic brakes leave to desire. They aren't as responsive as the rest.
I'm happy that they've decide to put the dropper post on this bike, but i wish that you were able to change the size.
3300$

Hardtail Bikes

These bikes are fun, poppy, and the cheaper of the categories. Thes are usually beginner bikes, but it doesnt mean that you cant go hard or fat on these. Having no shock adds a challenge, and these bikes are dialed for tricks.

Norco Torrent 1 HT This bike is very expensive for a hardtail. But i put it in this list because its so good!
The suspension alone is 1000$. Its good, provides good support, and will never break on you.
The sram NX 1 x 11 speed drivetrain is very good as seen in previous bikes. it has smooth shifting. At this price however, it should be better
The maxxis Minion tires are good in all trail conditions and are heavy duty.The plus tires help a lot.
3300$
Nukeproof This bike is fun, and poppy and its very god for jumps
it has a capable fork that wont break the bank if it needs replacements. The Rockshox revelations RT has 140mm has pleanty of travel for jumps.
The Shimano SLX are an upgrade over the shimano deore. They have resin pads which are better.
It has a dropper post which allows you to move the saddle out of riding position to get those jumps.
2049$

Downhill

These bikes are made to go fast downhill. Beware, they suck at climbing. They have a geometry that makes it impossible for you to get your weight in front of that tire in those climbs. They are meant to take heavy impacts and will nearly never break on you.

Bikes Image Details Price
Specializd Demo 8 Aluminium For a downhill bike, it has great value. The parts that are on it are excellent.
Its suspension is over 200mm of travel. Witha capable rockshox boxxer and a fox coil shock. Excellent for big impacts.
The big rotors on this bike are powerful, but they would be so much more with four pistons instead of two.
The tires on the bike are really not that great and not made for downhill at all. This is a 200$ upgrade.
3500$
Intense m16 For a limited time you can get the M16 expert build at a 2800$ discount. This is by far the best value for the money.
For this price you can have a carbon frame which is lighter and more playful.
you can also have full fox suspension which are plenty supportive and offer plenty of travel.
You get powerful guide R brakes with 203mm rotors on both wheels which are very hard to overheat.
2750$ USD
Commencal Supreme 27.5 V4.2 This bike is the most expensive of the bunch but still offers some good specs.
This bike has plenty of travel for those big impacts. 220mm shock and 200mm fork. Both are Rockshox and good quality.
The bike has by far the best chain tension system. it passes through the frame and stop chain slapping on the frame.
It has powerful brake with Guide RE brakes. 		4300$

Dirt Jump/Slopestyle

Dirt jump bikes and Slope style bikes are made for one thing, jumps and tricks. they have a small frame that helps you move the bike around in the air.
